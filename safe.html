Frequently Asked

Questions ?

01

Intervertebral Disc Prolapse

Dr. R Ravi research in spine problems has revealed that Spine Subluxation or retrolisthesis is a primary cause of intervertebral disc prolapse, often undetectable during diagnosis. Bony subluxation gradually disrupts the intervertebral disc, weakening the end plate, leading to bulging and compression of the spinal cord and nerves, resulting in radiculopathy.

02

Who gets Spinal Problems?

Anyone can get spinal problems, but they are more common in individuals who are older, have a family history of spinal issues, engage in repetitive activities that strain the spine, or have poor posture.

03

Diagnosing the Patients

Diagnosing spinal problems typically involves a physical examination, medical history review, and imaging tests such as X-rays, MRI, or CT scans to determine the exact nature and extent of the problem.

04

Intervertebral Disc Prolapse

Intervertebral disc prolapse, also known as a herniated disc, occurs when the soft inner gel of the disc leaks out through a tear in the outer layer, pressing on nerves and causing pain or other symptoms.
